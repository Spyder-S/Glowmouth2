GlowMouth's imaging system detects three categories of surface indicators visible under QLF (Quantitative Light-Induced Fluorescence) imaging: plaque-like film on tooth surfaces, changes in gum tissue that may signal early inflammation, and surface-level enamel characteristics. These are optical, surface-level signals — not subsurface measurements.

The system cannot detect cavities, bone loss, tartar below the gumline, or any condition requiring X-ray imaging or clinical probing. If you are concerned about any of those issues, see your dentist.

At launch, GlowMouth operates under FDA General Wellness Policy guidance. We are pursuing a Class II SaMD regulatory pathway over a multi-year timeline — that path is ongoing, not complete. Accuracy depends on image quality and the specific indicator. The app surfaces a confidence level (High / Medium / Low) with every result, and discards frames that fail blur or brightness quality checks before analysis runs. Every result is based on frames that passed those checks.

Our model was trained with input from practicing dentists and reviewed clinically during development. That said, the AI produces directional signals, not clinical measurements. We do not publish a specific accuracy percentage because that claim would require the kind of formal clinical validation we have not yet completed. A Low confidence result should be treated as inconclusive.

Yes, absolutely. GlowMouth does not replace dentist visits. Professional cleanings remove calculus that no home imaging device can address. Your dentist performs clinical examinations, takes X-rays, and identifies conditions — including bone loss, interproximal decay, and soft-tissue pathology — that are entirely outside the scope of what GlowMouth detects.

GlowMouth is designed to give you more information between visits, not to substitute for them. If GlowMouth flags a consistent concern over multiple scans, that is a signal to bring to your dentist — not something to manage on your own.

Your scan images and health data are encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). Images are stored only in your personal account. GlowMouth does not sell your data or share it with advertisers or data brokers.

You can delete any individual scan or your entire account at any time from the app's Settings section. All consent is explicit, versioned, and manageable — you choose what you share, and with whom.

Yes. Every scan generates a structured summary — your GlowScore, per-indicator readings, confidence level, and scan date — exportable as a PDF or shareable via the in-app partner portal. GlowMouth integrates directly with dental practices in the GlowMouth partner network.

Sharing is always optional and under your control. Your dentist has no obligation to act on GlowMouth data — we encourage you to discuss what the results mean with your provider directly.

The GlowScore is a single 1–100 number summarizing your scan for a given day. It is calculated from three indicator categories — plaque-like film, gum tissue signals, and enamel surface characteristics — each weighted by the severity of the detected signal. A higher score reflects fewer and milder surface indicators.

The score is a wellness signal, not a clinical measurement. It is most meaningful tracked over time: a consistent upward trend as you adjust your oral care routine is the signal to watch, not any single day's reading. The app provides 7-day, 30-day, and 90-day trend views.

GlowMouth is designed primarily for adults between 16 and 40 who want more visibility into their oral health between dental appointments — particularly people building new oral care habits who want feedback beyond what a manual brush and mirror can provide.

It is also relevant for people managing conditions that affect oral health (diabetes, dry mouth, acid reflux) or those with a history of cavities or gum disease who want closer monitoring. GlowMouth is not validated for children under 12. If you have active dental disease requiring treatment, GlowMouth is a complement to your care, not a substitute for it.

Smart toothbrushes measure brushing behavior — coverage, pressure, and duration — but do not analyze the condition of your oral tissues. GlowMouth uses QLF imaging to capture the actual state of your teeth and gums after brushing. That is a fundamentally different category of data.

A smart toothbrush tells you how you brushed. GlowMouth shows you what the result looks like — whether plaque film on your molars actually decreased, whether gum tissue color is changing over time, and where the most significant surface indicators are located. The two tools address different questions and can be used together.
