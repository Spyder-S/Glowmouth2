Dental Professionals & Advisors

Built with clinicians, not just for them.

GlowMouth's clinical foundation comes from practicing dentists and academic research partners who shaped the product from the ground up — not after the fact.

Clinical Advisors

Dentists who shaped the product.

Both of our clinical advisors are practicing dentists. Their input informed what GlowMouth measures, how the device is designed, and the regulatory pathway we are pursuing.

Dr. Surya Dhakar

DDS

Clinical Advisor — Device Design & Regulatory Pathway

Dr. Surya Dhakar is a practicing general dentist whose clinical work informed how GlowMouth's AI model is evaluated for real-world accuracy. She advised on what signals matter most during a patient exam, what the device's output should communicate to both patients and providers, and what requirements would be needed to support a Class II SaMD regulatory pathway.

Her clinical perspective shaped the GlowScore's three-indicator framework and the structured summary format that dentists receive when patients share results through the in-app partner portal.

Dr. Aditya Dhakar

Dental Professional

Clinical Advisor — Oral Health Research & Validation

Dr. Aditya Dhakar advises GlowMouth on the research and validation dimensions of the product. This includes how GlowMouth's QLF-based imaging methodology compares to existing peer-reviewed approaches in dental literature, how indicator categories are defined relative to established clinical terminology, and what a credible validation pathway toward Class II SaMD clearance would require.

Dr. Dhakar's input has shaped how GlowMouth frames its technical capabilities — ensuring the product communicates clearly without overstating what QLF-based surface imaging can determine at this stage.

Design Philosophy

GlowMouth is built with dental professionals, not just for them.

Consumer health technology often reaches the market before clinicians have had meaningful input into its design. GlowMouth takes the opposite approach. The clinical advisors and academic partners listed here were involved before the first prototype was built. They shaped what we measure, how we communicate results, and where we draw the line between what the product can and cannot determine.

Our advisors have pushed back on copy that overstated our capabilities, redefined indicator categories that were clinically imprecise, and shaped our regulatory roadmap toward a timeline that is honest about what Class II clearance requires. That friction is what a product like this needs.
